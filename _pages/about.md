Hi, I'm Rahil. I think of myself as a systems researcher. I am interested in why systems and protocols are shaped the way they are, and what their architectural choices enable, constrain, prevent, or imply.

I recently completed an MSc in Computer Science at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam and the University of Amsterdam, with a specialisation in Big Data Engineering. My thesis research at CWI Amsterdam introduced **Oblivious Bloom Filter Insertion (OBFI)**, a protocol for constructing encrypted Bloom filters while concealing insertion access patterns. I implemented OBFI as a distributed system and evaluated its computational and communication trade-offs against an alternative privacy-preserving protocol that solves the same problem.

My interests span three connected areas. The first is **systems and protocol design**, the architectural logic behind why particular designs succeed, where they break down, and why one approach may be preferable to another. The second is **reasoning about trade-offs under constraints**, particularly where security, privacy, scalability, and efficiency exist in tension rather than in isolation. The third is the **sociotechnical impact of systems**, what happens when technical architecture meets human judgement, organisational practice, and real-world decision-making.

I approach these questions by building and evaluating systems. I am drawn to work that moves between conceptual ideas, implementation, and empirical analysis: translating requirements into system designs, examining the assumptions behind them, and understanding how their behaviour changes under practical constraints.

Alongside my thesis, I have taken on projects across explainable machine learning, fraud detection, agentic AI systems, NLP and computational social science, energy-aware systems, and LLM evaluation. I treat these projects as a way to enter unfamiliar fields and understand their problems from the inside. I learn best by engaging with the literature, building a working system, and examining its behaviour empirically.

Before and alongside my research, I worked across enterprise data systems and university teaching. These experiences shaped my interest in interdisciplinary problems where reliable technical systems, human judgement, organisational requirements, and decision-making come together. They also taught me to communicate technical choices across different levels of expertise and to treat operational constraints as part of the research problem.

I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in systems-oriented and interdisciplinary research, particularly around **privacy-preserving systems**, **trustworthy AI**, **intelligent data systems**, and **sociotechnical computing**. Feel free to [get in touch](mailto:officialthingsrahil@gmail.com).
