Professional Summary

I think of myself as a systems researcher. I am interested in why systems and protocols are shaped the way they are, and what their architectural choices enable, constrain, prevent, or imply. My work spans three connected areas: systems and protocol design, reasoning about trade-offs under constraints (where security, privacy, scalability, and efficiency pull against each other), and the sociotechnical impact of systems. I bring 5+ years of combined industry, research, and teaching experience across enterprise data systems, privacy-preserving computation, explainable AI, and distributed systems. Currently seeking PhD opportunities in privacy-preserving systems, trustworthy AI, and sociotechnical computing.
