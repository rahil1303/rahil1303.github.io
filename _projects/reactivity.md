**VU Amsterdam, 2025**

## Overview

A computational framework for studying **political polarization, media framing, and audience reactivity** using large-scale YouTube transcript and comment data. The project operationalizes qualitative concepts — framing, reflectiveness, audience reactivity — into measurable computational features, enabling structured cross-media and cross-topic comparisons.

## Research Questions

- How do media narratives and audience responses interact across politically charged topics and news outlets?
- Can qualitative concepts like framing and reflectiveness be reliably operationalized as computational features?
- What patterns of reactivity emerge across different media sources and political events?

## System Design

- Collected and processed large-scale **YouTube transcript and comment data** across major political events and news outlets
- Designed a vector framework encoding **reactivity and reflectivity** as measurable dimensions of media and audience behaviour
- Built structured comparisons across topics and sources to support fair, context-aware analysis
- Applied NLP techniques to translate behavioural and engagement patterns into quantitative features
- Combined computational analysis with qualitative interpretation to study how narrative structure shapes audience response

## Key Contribution

The framework bridges qualitative media studies concepts and computational social science methods — making polarization dynamics measurable and comparable across contexts without reducing them to simple sentiment or engagement metrics.
